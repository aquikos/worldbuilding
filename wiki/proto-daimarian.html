Proto-Daimarian is the ancestor language of the languages found on the Daimarian peninsula, which includes languages such as Lir√≥. Proto-Daimarian's most notable features are the extensive case system, with 13 cases, and 2 numbers, which combine for 26 separate forms for every noun, and the incredibly complex verb system, which marks for three tenses, five aspects, four main moods, person, and number. This produces a very large amount of conjugations for each verb, but most have verb regular constructions, easing the process significantly.

Phonology

The phonology of Proto-Daimarian may be difficult for many people to learn, as there is both a voicing and aspiration distinction, a distinction between /r/ and /l/, a complete velar series. The vowel inventory is quite simple, as it is the "standard" five vowel system of /i/, /u/, /e/, /o/, and /a/.

Phonotactics

Proto-Daimarian is purely CV(C). Consonants always appear initially, and may appear finally. The only consonants that are not permitted finally is the aspirated series.

Orthography

The orthography of Proto-Daimarian is fairly straightforward. Because the aspirated series is marked with a post-consonantal ⟨h⟩, /x/ and /ɣ/ are romanized as ⟨x⟩ and ⟨h⟩, respectively. /ŋ/ is romanized as ng, as in English(and the cluster /ŋg/ is romanized as ⟨ngg⟩).

Grammar

On the most basic level, Proto-Daimarian has SVO word order(by default), which is familiar to speakers of many languages. Apart from demonstratives(and articles), all modifiers go after the noun.

The case system, while complex for speakers of many languages, pales in complexity compared to the verb system. The only saving grace is that both systems are very regular, and affixes are not fused, as Proto-Daimarian is agglutinative.
