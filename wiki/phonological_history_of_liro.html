Home · Liró · Phonological History

This list is a complete phonological history of Liró. While several stages separated Proto-Liro and Modern Liro, those stages are not mentioned; Only the time period in which the sound changes occurred is mentioned. One final note, is that dates are approximate, not exact. An important note is that the dates given are also approximate end times for the shifts, not beginning times. All years are in Earth years, not Neosaian years, unless otherwise noted.

~4.1 kya

Umlaut, Monophthongization
Umlaut triggering vowels(F) are: iː, eː, i, e
  • uCF, oCF, aCF > yCF, øCF, eCF

  • ei, ai > eː

  • eu, oi > øː

  • ou, au > oː

    • Note: UTVs only reduce if they triggered umlaut.
  • iː, eː, i, e > i, e, ɪ, ɛ

  • Diphthongs also monophthongized before Umlaut actually occurred, so soulei > soːleː > søːle
