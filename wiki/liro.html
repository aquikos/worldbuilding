Liró is one of the most widely spoken languages on the planet Neosaia. While the English name is a direct loan, it is usually spelled ⟨Liro⟩ instead of ⟨Liró⟩, and is usually pronounced /liɹoʊ/ instead of /liɾo/.

History

See: Phonological History of Liró for sound changes.

Liró is descended from Proto-Daimarian, which was spoken about 6,000 years before Modern Liró. Proto-Daimarian split into three distinct branches: North, South, and East. Liró is descended from the Eastern branch. The Eastern branch split into three more branches: Riparian, Central, and Peninsular Daimarian. Peninsular split into a north, east, south, west, and central dialects. Eastern Peninsular gave rise to several languages, but Old Liró is the only important one in this situation.

In summary, it went: Proto-Daimarian > Peninsular Daimarian > East Peninsular > Old Liró

Phonology

The phonology of Modern Standard Liró(MSL) is quite easy for speakers of most languages to learn. Areas of trouble may include the postalveolar fricatives, /ʃ/ and /ʒ/, the alveolar tap, /ɾ/, the distinction between close-mid vowels and open-mid vowels, and the 6 diphthongs. The voiceless plosives /p/, /t/, and /k/ may be aspirated as in English, as no distinction is made, but it would sound bizarre to a native speaker. English speakers in particular may attempt to pronounce /e/ and /o/ as diphthongs /eɪ/ and /oʊ/, but this is not optional, as is would conflict with pre-existing diphthongs.

Phonotactics

Liró is almost purely (C)V. Consonants are required non-word initially, but may be absent in the initial position.

Orthography

Grammar

Liró still maintains the heavy case system found in Proto-Daimarian, which allows for fairly free word order. Regardless, the default word order is SVO.

In Liró, primary stress always falls initially, and secondary stress always falls on the ultimate or penultimate syllable. What determines secondary stress placement is syllable count. Monosyllabic and bisyllabic words only have primary stress, trisyllabic words have ultimate stress, and quadrisyllabic or greater have penultimate stress.
